## Care by Fabric Type

### Cotton

Cotton is durable and easy to care for.

**Washing**:
- Machine wash warm or cold
- Use regular detergent
- Separate whites from colors

**Drying**:
- Tumble dry medium
- Remove promptly to reduce wrinkles
- Can be line dried

**Ironing**:
- Iron on high heat while slightly damp
- Use steam for best results

**Tips**:
- Pre-treat stains before washing
- Turn dark colors inside out
- Avoid bleach on colored items

### Wool

Wool requires gentle care to maintain its quality.

**Washing**:
- Hand wash in cold water preferred
- Use wool-specific detergent
- Machine wash only on wool/delicate cycle

**Drying**:
- Lay flat to dry on a towel
- Never tumble dry (causes shrinking)
- Reshape while damp

**Ironing**:
- Use low heat with pressing cloth
- Steam is preferred over direct contact

**Tips**:
- Store with cedar to prevent moths
- Let wool rest 24 hours between wears
- Fold knitwear, don't hang

### Silk

Silk is delicate and requires special attention.

**Washing**:
- Hand wash in cold water
- Use mild detergent or baby shampoo
- Dry cleaning recommended for structured items

**Drying**:
- Roll in towel to remove excess water
- Hang or lay flat to dry
- Keep away from direct sunlight

**Ironing**:
- Iron on low heat while slightly damp
- Use pressing cloth
- Iron on reverse side

**Tips**:
- Test colorfastness before washing
- Avoid perfume/deodorant contact
- Store in breathable garment bags

### Synthetic Fabrics (Polyester, Nylon, Spandex)

**Washing**:
- Machine wash cold or warm
- Turn inside out to protect surface
- Use gentle detergent

**Drying**:
- Tumble dry low or air dry
- Remove promptly to prevent static
- Avoid high heat (causes damage)

**Ironing**:
- Low heat only
- Use pressing cloth
- Steam with caution

**Tips**:
- Wash athletic wear after each use
- Avoid fabric softeners (affects moisture-wicking)
- Air out between washes when possible

### Denim

**Washing**:
- Wash infrequently to preserve color
- Turn inside out
- Cold water, gentle cycle

**Drying**:
- Air dry preferred
- Tumble dry low if needed

**Tips**:
- Spot clean when possible
- Freeze jeans to kill bacteria between washes
- Raw denim: wait 6 months before first wash

## Stain Removal Guide

### Common Stains

| Stain Type | Treatment |
|------------|-----------|
| Coffee/Tea | Cold water + dish soap, soak 15 min |
| Red Wine | Salt + club soda, blot don't rub |
| Grease/Oil | Dish soap + warm water, or baking soda |
| Blood | Cold water only (hot sets stain) |
| Ink | Rubbing alcohol, blot with cloth |
| Grass | White vinegar + water, soak |
| Makeup | Makeup remover or dish soap |

### General Stain Rules

1. **Act quickly**: Fresh stains are easier to remove
2. **Blot, don't rub**: Rubbing spreads the stain
3. **Cold water first**: Hot water can set protein stains
4. **Test first**: Test cleaning solution on hidden area
5. **Patience**: Repeat treatment if needed before drying

## Storage Tips

### Seasonal Storage
- Clean items before storing
- Use breathable garment bags (not plastic)
- Add cedar blocks or lavender sachets
- Store in cool, dry location

### Hanging vs. Folding
**Hang**:
- Blazers and suits
- Dresses
- Button-down shirts
- Coats

**Fold**:
- Knitwear and sweaters
- T-shirts
- Jeans
- Activewear
